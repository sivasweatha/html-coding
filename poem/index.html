nameless poem about the arts

On those canvases,
I painted not the beauty of the nature,
but tried to find amidst my palette
the shades that you painted the world.
On those sheets, I wrote poems,
not about inebriating love,
but to capture the feeling of being you.
In those melodies, I sang regardless of hour,
not to express the joy of life,
but to let the song of your soul
ring through all the corners of the world.
In those dances,
I mimicked not the movement of the wind,
but fought with my muscles
to hold a candle to the gracefulness of your gait.
All those years,
I lived not the vivacious days of youth,
but the passion that was fueled by the thought of
merging with you.
The end.
